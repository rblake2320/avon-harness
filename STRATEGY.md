# Avon Copilot — Business Strategy
*Last updated: June 24, 2026*

---

## What We Are Building and Why It Wins

Avon Copilot is a subscription AI assistant built specifically for Avon Representatives
with The Avon Company (US entity, LG H&H). The competitive edge is the same as MK
Copilot: specificity. Generic AI tools don't know Avon's campaign-based pricing, don't
know the $40 minimum-per-campaign floor, don't warn reps about the guest checkout
commission loss, and will happily generate income claims that violate FTC law.

There is one additional factor that makes Avon *more* urgent than MK: Avon already
tried to build their own AI assistant (Anele, in the Avon ON app, v3.1.3 March 2026)
and it's broken — it argues with users about their own account information and has a
3.96/5 rating from 26,000 reviews. The need is proven. The demand is unmet. The
official solution is failing. We are the answer that Avon Inc. couldn't build.

## The Money: How We Make It

### Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Who It's For |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Solo** | $9.99/mo or $89/yr | Individual Avon rep |
| **Leader** | $19.99/mo or $179/yr | Leader + up to 10 downline reps |

**$9.99 is the right Solo price for Avon.** The Mary Kay market can support $9.99–$14.99.
Avon reps net $50–150/month after costs and are more price-sensitive. $9.99 is "less than
two lipsticks a month" — a frame that works in a beauty product context. Annual at $89
breaks down to $7.42/mo, which gets past almost all price objections.

| 500 | $4,995 | $59,940 |

The US rep base is difficult to size precisely (last public figure was 250K from 2019;

That's the floor on a genuine push. Leader tier and annual subs are the multipliers.

### What Drives the Number Up

1. **Leader conversion.** An Avon Leader has a downline. Sell one Leader on the Leader
   tier and 5–10 reps come with them. This is the highest-leverage channel.
2. **Seasonal spikes.** Avon's Bug Guard business is enormous in spring/summer. New reps
   join seasonally. Run trial campaigns in March–April to capture spring joiners before
   they form habits with other tools.
3. **The "Anele replacement" narrative.** Avon's broken AI assistant is a live pain point
   with documented public complaints. "We're what Avon's AI should have been" is a
   positioning line that writes itself and doesn't require explaining the category.
4. **Cross-sell to MK.** Some reps run both brands. A rep already on MK Copilot for $9.99
   can add Avon Copilot for $5.99/mo (bundle discount). Low incremental cost to us;
   real value to dual-brand reps.

## The Value Proposition: Why They Pay and Why They Stay

### What We Replace (and what that costs them now)

| ChatGPT Plus | Social content, message drafting | $20.00 |
| Buffer or Later | Social scheduling (content still manual) | $15.00 |

| AV4 Software | Order history per customer | varies |
| **Total** | | **$45+/mo** |

**We cost $9.99/mo and handle the content and customer relationship work.**

We do not replace Direct Sidekick for tax/accounting — we're explicit about that.
But we replace the $35/mo they spend on content and customer messaging tools that
don't know anything about Avon.

The pitch: *"You're spending $35 a month on tools that make you do all the Avon thinking
yourself. We cost $10 and already know Avon."*

### What We Do That Nothing Else Does

1. **Campaign pricing discipline.** No other AI tool knows never to quote Avon prices.
   ChatGPT will confidently tell a customer that the Anew Ultimate Day Cream is $X —
   wrong for this campaign, possibly wrong by $10–15, and it destroys credibility.
   We never invent a price. We always direct to the current campaign brochure.

2. **Guest checkout coaching.** The #1 documented revenue leak for Avon reps is guest
   checkout — when a customer checks out without creating an account, Avon keeps the
   commission. No other tool teaches reps to address this on every customer interaction.
   Our follow-up writer surfaces this automatically when generating customer messages.

3. **The $40 floor and 3-campaign warning.** Reps who don't hit $40 earn zero. Three
   campaigns without an order and the account deactivates. Our sales coach skill knows
   these rules and coaches around them — "you need $X more this campaign to earn
   commission" is something no generic AI can calculate.

4. **FTC compliance.** Avon has no official US Income Disclosure Statement. That makes
   every income claim from a rep technically unsubstantiated. Our 58-pattern income
   claim filter catches and blocks this before it can be sent or saved. No other rep
   tool does this.

5. **Skin profile in the CRM.** Anew is the flagship skincare line with 5 sub-lines
   for different concerns. When a rep runs a skin analysis and we capture undertone and
   Fitzpatrick type, the follow-up writer knows which Anew sub-line to recommend for
   that specific customer — without the rep having to remember it.

### Why They Don't Cancel

Avon reps cancel tools when the tool doesn't make them money. The tools that survive
are the ones tied to a sale they can point to.

Our daily suggestions endpoint surfaces the 5 most overdue customers every day. A rep
who contacts 3 of those 5 and gets one reorder has paid for 2 months of subscription
from one sale. Make that visible: after every reorder-prompted follow-up, show the rep
"this contact suggestion may have recovered a sale."

The second retention driver is Anew product knowledge. Reps leave money on the table
because they don't confidently know which Anew sub-line fits which customer. Our
product_qa skill makes every rep sound like they've used every Anew product personally.
Confidence in product knowledge = more recommendations = more sales = rep stays
subscribed because it's working.

## How We Grow

### Phase 1 — The "Anele Is Broken" Opening (Months 1–2)

The Avon ON app's AI assistant Anele has active, recent, public complaints. This is
an organic marketing opportunity. Reps searching "Avon AI app not working" or complaining
in Facebook groups about Anele are already in the mental state of "I need a better AI
for my Avon business."

**Tactic:** Create content that shows up in those searches. A YouTube video titled
"Avon's AI assistant Anele is broken — here's what actually works" captures high-intent
reps at zero ad spend. This is not attacking Avon — it's meeting reps where they already
are.

### Phase 2 — The Facebook Rep Community (Months 1–4)

Avon rep Facebook groups are large and active. The key groups:
- Avon USA Representatives (direct selling group)
- Avon Reps & Leaders (larger community)
- Campaign-specific groups that form each cycle

**Tactic:** Do not run ads into these groups — they'll be removed. Instead, identify
the 20–30 most active voices in each group (the reps who answer questions, share tips,
create content). Offer them a free Leader account for 90 days. Active community voices
who genuinely find the tool useful will mention it. That mention is worth 100 ads.

### Phase 3 — Seasonal Acquisition (March–April Each Year)

Avon's spring campaign cycle brings new reps in specifically because of Bug Guard season.
New reps have no tool habits yet. A targeted onboarding offer ("Free first month,
specifically designed for Avon's spring campaign") captures them before Teamzy or
generic AI tools do.

**Build:** A Bug Guard-specific onboarding flow inside the product — 3 pre-built social
posts for Bug Guard, 2 customer message templates for outdoor lifestyle customers, a skin
analysis prompt optimized for Skin So Soft body care products. New spring rep opens the
app, sees Avon-specific content that works immediately, converts to paid.

### Phase 4 — Data as the Sales Pitch to Avon Corporate (Month 12+)

At scale, we have data that Avon Inc. doesn't have: what questions their reps ask most,
what compliance events we catch, what the aggregate skin profile looks like across their
customer base. This data has enterprise value.

A conversation with The Avon Company (LG H&H) at month 12 could go in several directions:
- Official integration into AvonNow (licensing deal — replaces broken Anele)
- Sponsored data partnership
- Acquisition

None of this is the primary plan — subscriber revenue is. But the data moat we're
building has value beyond subscriptions and the enterprise path becomes real at scale.

## What We Don't Do (and Why That's Right)

**We don't do accounting/tax.** Direct Sidekick owns the Avon accounting niche and is
good at it. We tell reps: "For taxes and income tracking, Direct Sidekick is the right
tool. We handle everything that touches customers and content."

**We don't do order management.** AvonNow is not accessible to us. We don't try to
replicate ordering, tracking, or back-office functions. Those are platform problems.

**We don't fix guest checkout at the platform level.** We can't change Avon's checkout
system. We can coach reps to address it on every customer interaction. That's our lane.

**We don't promise income.** We help reps have better conversations with customers.
Better conversations lead to more sales. That's the chain we can defend. "Our tool
helps you follow up better" is not an income claim. "Our tool will make you $X" is.

## Competitive Positioning: Why They Don't Ask "Why Bother With You?"

The tools an Avon rep is currently paying for all share one problem: **they don't
know Avon.** Teamzy doesn't know the $40 campaign minimum. Buffer doesn't know Anew's
sub-lines. ChatGPT doesn't know campaign pricing changes every two weeks.

Every tool they're currently using requires the rep to do the Avon thinking and then
use the tool to execute. We reverse that. We do the Avon thinking so the rep doesn't
have to.

The "why bother" question only arises when a tool is redundant. We're not redundant
with anything they have — we're the Avon-specific intelligence layer that makes
everything else they do more effective.
